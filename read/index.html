Introduction to Physics

Chapter 1: The Fundamentals

Physics is the natural science that studies matter, its motion and behavior through space and time, and the related entities of energy and force. Physics is one of the most fundamental scientific disciplines, and its main goal is to understand how the universe behaves.

1.1 Scientific Method

The scientific method is a systematic way of learning about the world around us and answering questions. The key steps in the scientific method are:

  • Observation: Noticing and describing events or processes in a careful, orderly way.
  • Hypothesis: A testable explanation for observations.
  • Experiment: A procedure designed to test a hypothesis.
  • Analysis: Interpreting the data from the experiment and drawing conclusions.
  • Conclusion: Determining whether the hypothesis was supported or refuted.

Physics uses the scientific method to develop theories and laws that describe the behavior of the physical world. These theories are continually tested through observation and experimentation.
